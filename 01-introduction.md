This chapter orients you to the modalities, pathways and reporting frameworks you will use daily. Master these foundations before progressing to lesion-specific chapters.

{% tab chapter-1 Clinical Context %}

## Why Breast Imaging Matters

- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in UK women; early detection through screening and timely symptomatic assessment improves survival and enables breast-conserving surgery.
- Breast radiology underpins population screening, symptomatic "one-stop" clinics, pre-operative staging (including the axilla), and treatment surveillance.

## Indications and Service Models

- **Screening (asymptomatic):**
  - NHSBSP routinely offers <<3-yearly mammography to women aged 50–70>>; extension in some units 47–73.
  - High-risk screening (e.g., BRCA1/2, TP53, prior mantle radiotherapy): <<annual MRI ± mammography>> according to age and risk stratification.
- **Symptomatic:**
  - New persistent lump, focal pain with a finding, skin/nipple change, discharge (especially unilateral, spontaneous, bloodstained), infection/abscess, implant problems.
  - Men with gynaecomastia or suspicious male breast lump.
- **Post-treatment:** baseline and annual surveillance per surgical/oncology protocols.

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- <<Ultrasound is first-line>> for pregnant or lactating women with focal symptoms.
- Mammography is acceptable when clinically indicated, with abdominal shielding.
- <<Gadolinium is generally avoided in pregnancy>>; CEM is avoided in pregnancy.

## Modality Contraindications

| Modality | Key precautions |
|---|---|
| Mammography | No absolute contraindication; shield abdomen in pregnancy |
| Ultrasound | None (non-ionising) |
| MRI | Non-compatible devices, severe claustrophobia; <<avoid gadolinium in pregnancy>>, check renal function |
| CEM | Iodinated contrast allergy, uncontrolled thyrotoxicosis, significant renal impairment; avoid in pregnancy |
| Biopsy | Allergy, bleeding diathesis/anticoagulation, infection risk |

## Core Modalities — Practical Essentials

### Digital Mammography and DBT

- Compression spreads tissue to reduce superimposition, motion and dose. DBT reconstructs 1 mm slices, improving conspicuity of masses and architectural distortion.
- **Standard views:** Craniocaudal (CC) and Mediolateral oblique (MLO).
- **Adequacy checks:**
  - <<Posterior nipple line (PNL) on CC within 1 cm of PNL on MLO>>.
  - Pectoralis muscle to or below nipple level on MLO; inframammary fold included.
  - Nipple in profile on at least one view; no skin folds; posterior/medial tissue visualised on CC.
- **Technical figures:**
  - Compression force typically <<11–18 daN>>.
  - <<Average glandular dose ~1–3 mGy per view; total four-view study ~3–5 mGy>>.
  - Magnification for calcifications: geometric magnification <<1.5–1.8× with a 0.1 mm focal spot>>.

### Ultrasound

- High-frequency linear probe (7–15 MHz; up to 18 MHz for superficial/axillary work).
- Document clock-face position, distance from nipple, depth, and three orthogonal measurements.
- **Sonographic descriptors:** shape, orientation (parallel vs non-parallel), margin, echo pattern, posterior features, surrounding changes, vascularity.
- **Lymph nodes:** normal cortex <<≤3 mm with central fatty hilum and hilar vascularity>>; suspicious features include focal cortical bulge, cortex >3 mm, round shape, loss of hilum, or non-hilar flow.

### Breast MRI

- Dynamic contrast-enhanced T1 fat-suppressed sequences after <<0.1 mmol/kg gadolinium chelate>>; T2 and DWI (b-values typically 0 and 800–1000 s/mm²).
- **Indications:** problem-solving, extent of disease in dense breasts or invasive lobular cancer, multifocality assessment, neoadjuvant monitoring, implant integrity (non-contrast), high-risk screening.
- **Kinetics:** Type I persistent, Type II plateau, Type III washout (increasing suspicion).
- Report background parenchymal enhancement (minimal/mild/moderate/marked) as it affects sensitivity.

### Contrast-Enhanced Mammography

- Dual-energy acquisition after iodinated contrast; subtraction images display enhancement analogous to MRI.
- Useful in dense breasts and where MRI is unavailable; same contrast precautions as CT.

## Clinic Workflows

### Symptomatic One-Stop — Triple Assessment

- **P:** clinical examination.
- **M:** mammography if age ≥35 (earlier if red flags).
- **U:** ultrasound in all ages with focal symptoms; first-line if <35, pregnant or lactating.
- Tissue sampling as indicated (core biopsy first-line for solid masses and calcifications; FNA for cysts/abscesses or nodes).

### Screening Assessment Clinic

- Review screening films with priors → additional views (spot compression, magnification, DBT) → targeted ultrasound → image-guided biopsy in the same visit if appropriate.

## Image-Guided Procedures Primer

- **Core needle biopsy (CNB):** 14G common; vacuum-assisted biopsy (VAB; 7–10G) for calcifications, architectural distortion, small masses, radial scars and B3 lesions.
- **Guidance:** ultrasound first when visible; stereotactic/DBT for mammography-only findings; MRI-guided for MRI-only lesions.
- Place a marker clip after sampling; perform post-procedure mammogram for calcifications.
- **Pre-operative localisation:** wire, magnetic seed, radar reflector or carbon track per local availability.

{% tab chapter-1 Reporting Frameworks %}

## Breast Density (BI-RADS a–d)

- **a:** Almost entirely fatty.
- **b:** Scattered fibroglandular density.
- **c:** Heterogeneously dense (may obscure small masses).
- **d:** Extremely dense (<<lowers mammographic sensitivity and modestly increases cancer risk>>).

## Mammographic Descriptors

- **Mass:** shape (round/oval/irregular), margin (circumscribed/obscured/microlobulated/indistinct/spiculated), density.
- **Calcifications:**
  - *Typically benign:* skin, vascular, coarse/popcorn, large rod-like secretory, round/punctate, dystrophic, milk of calcium.
  - *Suspicious:* amorphous, coarse heterogeneous, fine pleomorphic, fine linear/linear branching.
  - *Distribution:* diffuse, regional, <<grouped (≥5 calcifications within 1 cm)>>, linear, segmental.
- **Asymmetries:** asymmetry (one view), focal asymmetry (two views), global, developing.
- **Architectural distortion:** focal disruption with spicules or retraction without a definite mass.

## BI-RADS Assessment Categories

| Category | Meaning | Management |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Incomplete | Additional imaging / prior comparison |
| 1 | Negative | Routine screening |
| 2 | Benign | Routine screening |
| 3 | Probably benign | <<<2% malignancy; short-interval follow-up (typically 6, 12, 24 months)>> |
| 4A | Low suspicion | <<2–10% malignancy; tissue diagnosis>> |
| 4B | Moderate suspicion | <<10–50% malignancy; tissue diagnosis>> |
| 4C | High suspicion | <<50–95% malignancy; tissue diagnosis>> |
| 5 | Highly suggestive | <<≥95% malignancy; tissue diagnosis>> |
| 6 | Known malignancy | Treatment planning |

## UK Symptomatic Scoring

- Mammography (M1–M5) and ultrasound (U1–U5): 1 normal, 2 benign, 3 indeterminate, 4 suspicious, 5 malignant — used alongside BI-RADS in symptomatic clinics.

## Quality, Safety and Audit

- Always place skin markers on palpable lumps, scars, moles and inverted nipples.
- Check pregnancy status; shield abdomen for mammography.
- Confirm radiology–pathology concordance at MDT; <<discordant benign biopsies after suspicious imaging mandate repeat biopsy or excision>>.
- **NHSBSP performance markers:**
  - Recall: prevalent round <<acceptable ≤7%, target ≤5%>>; incident round <<acceptable ≤5%, target ≤3%>>.
  - Cancer detection: prevalent <<≥8/1000>>, incident <<≥6/1000>>.

## Common Pitfalls

- Summation artefact mimicking mass → use spot compression / DBT.
- Skin lesions mimicking mass → use skin markers; look for lucent centre.
- Missed retroareolar lesions → ensure nipple in profile; dedicated retroareolar ultrasound.
- Dense breast masking → DBT, targeted ultrasound, consider CEM/MRI.
- Overcalling normal nodes → assess cortex (≤3 mm), shape, hilum and Doppler.

## Structured Read — Six Steps

1. Check image quality, positioning and density (a–d).
2. Systematic review: masses → calcifications → asymmetries → architectural distortion → skin/nipple/axilla.
3. Correlate with prior imaging and clinical findings.
4. Targeted ultrasound for any mammographic or clinical concern.
5. Assign BI-RADS / M / U category with a clear recommendation.
6. If biopsied, place clip, perform post-procedure mammogram for calcifications, and arrange MDT/follow-up.

## Key Take-Aways

- Breast radiology is built on the triple assessment (clinical, imaging, pathology) — always reconcile all three at MDT and pursue radiology–pathology concordance.
- Use ultrasound liberally for correlation; it is first-line in young, pregnant and lactating women, and essential for axillary assessment.
- Master positioning adequacy (PNL within 1 cm; pectoralis to nipple level) and the BI-RADS lexicon before chasing subtle pathology.
- BI-RADS categories drive management: 3 = short-interval follow-up; 4–5 = tissue diagnosis; 0 means more imaging is needed.
- Respect modality limits — dense breasts mask cancers on mammography; consider DBT, CEM or MRI when indicated.
- Safety first: check pregnancy status, contrast contraindications, anticoagulation and implant compatibility before every study or procedure.
