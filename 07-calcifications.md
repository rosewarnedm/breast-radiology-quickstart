Microcalcifications are the most frequent mammographic presentation of ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) and require systematic morphological and distributional analysis. Accurate description using BI-RADS lexicon, appropriate magnification work-up, and image-guided vacuum-assisted biopsy underpin safe management. This chapter covers classification, benign mimics, suspicious patterns, and procedural considerations.

{% tab chapter-7 Classification %}

## Approach to Calcifications on Mammography

Calcifications must be evaluated for **morphology**, **distribution**, **extent**, and **change over time**. Use the BI-RADS lexicon and assign a definitive category with a clear recommendation.

## Technical Work-up

- Confirm on both CC and MLO; compare with priors (<<stability over ≥2 years strongly favours benignity>>).
- Exclude artefact: deodorant, talc, ECG leads, skin lesions.
- Diagnostic views:
  - **Spot compression magnification CC and MLO** — definitive for morphology.
  - **True lateral (ML/LM) magnification** — required to assess layering (milk of calcium).
- Magnification technique:
  - <<Magnification factor 1.5–1.8×>>
  - <<Small focal spot (0.1–0.2 mm) with an air gap; no grid required>>
  - Firm, even compression to reduce motion and overlap.
- DBT improves conspicuity and localisation but **does not replace magnification views** for morphological assessment.

## BI-RADS Morphology

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Typically benign | Skin (lucent centre), vascular, coarse popcorn, eggshell/rim, dystrophic, milk of calcium, secretory rod-like |
| Probably benign | Round (>0.5 mm), punctate (≤0.5 mm) |
| Suspicious | Amorphous, coarse heterogeneous, fine pleomorphic, fine linear/linear branching |

## BI-RADS Distribution

- **Diffuse/scattered** — random, often bilateral.
- **Regional** — large volume (>2 cm), not conforming to a duct.
- **Grouped/clustered** — <<≥5 calcifications within ~1 cm or ≤1 cm³>>.
- **Linear** — arranged in a line; suggests ductal deposition.
- **Segmental** — follows a ductal/lobar system towards the nipple (**strongly suspicious**).

## BI-RADS Risk Thresholds

| Category | Likelihood of malignancy |
|---|---|
| BI-RADS 2 | Definitely benign |
| BI-RADS 3 | <<≤2%>> |
| BI-RADS 4A | <<>2–10%>> |
| BI-RADS 4B | <<>10–50%>> |
| BI-RADS 4C | <<>50–95%>> |
| BI-RADS 5 | <<≥95%>> |

## Reporting Template

“Right breast, UOQ, 10 o’clock, 60 mm from nipple. Fine pleomorphic calcifications in a segmental distribution, maximal span 28 mm CC and 25 mm MLO. No associated mass or distortion. **BI-RADS 4C** — recommend stereotactic vacuum-assisted biopsy and clip placement.”

Always include: morphology, distribution, **extent in mm on both views**, location, category, and recommendation.

{% tab chapter-7 Benign Calcifications %}

## Typically Benign Entities (BI-RADS 2)

### Skin (dermal) calcifications
- Lucent centres; clustered around skin pores; often on medial breast.
- Confirm with **tangential view** or skin marker.

### Vascular calcifications
- Parallel “tram-track” linear calcifications along a vessel; common in older or diabetic patients.

### Coarse “popcorn”
- <<>2–3 mm popcorn-like calcifications in an involuting fibroadenoma>>.

### Rim/eggshell
- Thin curvilinear rim around an oil cyst or area of fat necrosis; correlate with trauma/surgery.

### Dystrophic
- Large (often >0.5 mm), irregular; appear **1–3 years** after surgery, trauma or radiotherapy.

### Milk of calcium
- Sediment within microcysts; **gravity dependent**.
- Appearance: amorphous/round on CC, **crescentic or “teacup” layering on true lateral magnification**.
- Always obtain a true lateral if suspected.

### Secretory (plasma cell mastitis)
- Smooth, thick, **rod-like calcifications >1 mm**, ductal, often bilateral in postmenopausal women.
- Distinguish from malignant fine linear/branching, which are thinner, irregular, of varying calibre.

## Probably Benign (BI-RADS 3)

- Grouped round/punctate without suspicious distribution.
- Management: <<short-interval mammographic follow-up at 6 months, then 12 and 24 months to document ≥2-year stability>>; biopsy if change occurs.
- Likelihood of malignancy <<≤2%>>.

## Avoiding Overcalls and Mimics

| Pitfall | Avoidance strategy |
|---|---|
| Deodorant/talc artefact | Ask patient to remove; repeat views |
| Skin calcifications mistaken for parenchymal | Look for lucent centres; tangential view ± skin marker |
| Milk of calcium called amorphous | Always obtain **true lateral magnification** |
| Secretory called malignant | Smooth, thick, bilateral, ductal — benign |
| Post-treatment dystrophic confused with recurrence | Correlate with surgical/radiotherapy history and prior imaging |

## Suspicious Morphologies (BI-RADS 4–5)

| Morphology | Typical size | Typical category |
|---|---|---|
| Amorphous | Too small to define | 4A–4B |
| Coarse heterogeneous | 0.5–1 mm, irregular | 4B |
| Fine pleomorphic | <<<0.5 mm>>, varying shapes/sizes | 4B–4C |
| Fine linear / linear branching | Thin, irregular, tapering/branching (casting) | 4C–5 |

- **Segmental or linear distribution** of suspicious morphology markedly raises the category.
- **Fine linear branching calcifications in a segmental distribution** are typically <<BI-RADS 5 (≥95% likelihood of malignancy)>> and often represent **high-grade/comedo DCIS**.

## Underlying Pathology

- **Low–intermediate grade DCIS**: amorphous or fine pleomorphic; grouped/segmental.
- **High-grade/comedo DCIS**: fine linear/branching; segmental, often extensive.
- **Invasive carcinoma**: may present with calcifications, usually with an associated mass or distortion.
- **ADH**: amorphous/fine pleomorphic; <<upgrade to DCIS/invasive cancer in ~20–30% on excision, particularly with limited core sampling>>.

## Biopsy: Stereotactic / DBT-guided Vacuum-Assisted Biopsy (VAB)

**Indications**
- BI-RADS 4 or 5 calcifications.
- BI-RADS 3 that increase, change, or are discordant.
- Clinical–radiological discordance.

**Pre-procedure checks**
- Consent, allergies, anticoagulation review per local policy.
- Pregnancy status if relevant.
- Assess ability to lie prone/upright; rule out severe kyphosis or uncorrected coagulopathy.

**Procedure**
1. Position breast in fenestrated paddle; confirm lesion within window.
2. Target centre of cluster; confirm coordinates on stereo-pair or DBT slices.
3. Infiltrate local anaesthetic.
4. Use a <<vacuum-assisted needle (typically 7–10G) for microcalcifications>> to maximise yield and reduce ADH/DCIS underestimation.
5. Obtain <<12–24 contiguous cores>> bracketing the target.
6. **Specimen radiography immediately** to confirm calcifications within cores — essential for sampling adequacy.
7. **Deploy a marker clip** and perform a limited post-biopsy mammogram (CC + ML) documenting clip position.
8. Haemostasis, dressing, aftercare instructions.

**If calcifications are absent on specimen radiography:** alert the team — consider additional sampling or repeat biopsy.

## Radiology–Pathology Correlation

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| Benign concordant (e.g. fibrocystic) | Routine follow-up |
| B3 / high-risk (ADH, radial scar with calcifications) | MDT discussion ± vacuum-assisted excision or surgical excision |
| DCIS / invasive cancer | MDT for surgical planning ± SLNB |
| Benign discordant | Repeat VAB/VAE or surgical excision |

## Localisation for Surgery

- Wire or seed (magnetic/radiofrequency) at the most suspicious/central calcifications.
- **Bracket if extent is large** to guide complete excision.
- Always report **maximum calcification span (mm) on both CC and MLO**.
- Intra-operative **specimen radiography** confirms removal; residual calcifications on post-operative mammography may indicate incomplete excision.

## Special Scenarios

- **Men**: <<clustered microcalcifications in men are suspicious and warrant biopsy>> (gynaecomastia rarely calcifies).
- **Pregnancy/lactation**: mammography is safe with shielding; biopsy if suspicious morphology/distribution.
- **Post-treatment breast**: expect dystrophic calcifications after 1–3 years; remain alert for new segmental microcalcifications remote from scar.

## Key Take-Aways

- Always describe calcifications by **morphology, distribution, extent (mm on both views), and change**, then assign a definitive BI-RADS category with a clear recommendation.
- **Spot compression magnification CC, MLO and true lateral** views remain the gold standard for morphological assessment — DBT improves conspicuity but does not replace them.
- **Fine linear/branching, segmentally distributed calcifications** are typically BI-RADS 5 and represent high-grade DCIS until proven otherwise.
- **Stereotactic vacuum-assisted biopsy with 7–10G needle, ≥12 cores, immediate specimen radiography, and marker clip placement** is the standard approach for suspicious microcalcifications.
- **Radiology–pathology concordance** is mandatory: absence of calcifications on specimen radiography or discordant benign histology warrants repeat sampling or excision.
- Benign mimics — dermal, vascular, milk of calcium, secretory, dystrophic — can be confidently recognised with appropriate views and clinical correlation, avoiding unnecessary biopsy.
