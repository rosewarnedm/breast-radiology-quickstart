Breast ultrasound is a cornerstone of triple assessment, complementing mammography in lesion characterisation, axillary staging, and procedural guidance. It is first-line in women under 30, in pregnancy and lactation, and in men. A systematic technique with the BI-RADS Ultrasound Lexicon ensures reproducible, actionable reports.

{% tab chapter-3 Technique %}

## Equipment and Presets

- **Transducer:** high-frequency linear array, <<12–18 MHz preferred; 7–12 MHz for deep lesions or large breasts>>.
- **Presets:** tissue harmonic imaging (THI) ON, spatial compound imaging, dynamic range 50–70 dB, focus at or just deep to lesion.
- **Depth:** include skin to pectoral fascia/pleura.
- **Pressure:** minimal compression when assessing cysts and vascularity.

## Doppler and Elastography

- **Doppler optimisation:** low PRF (0.5–1.0 kHz), low wall filter, colour gain just below noise threshold; power Doppler for slow flow.
- **Shear-wave elastography (SWE):** <<mean velocity >3.5 m/s or mean elasticity >80 kPa is supportive of suspicion>>, with overlap in benign fibrosis, scar, and mastitis.
- Elastography is a supportive adjunct only — **never upgrade a clearly benign lesion on elastography alone**.

## Patient Positioning

- Supine or supine-oblique with ipsilateral arm raised behind the head; wedge under shoulder optional.
- For axillary scanning: arm abducted 90 degrees or hand behind head.
- Generous gel to improve coupling.

## Systematic Scanning

- Scan in **radial and anti-radial planes** centred on the nipple.
- Overlapping sweeps to cover retroareolar region, subareolar ducts, peripheral tissue, and tail of Spence.
- For each lesion: orthogonal images, BI-RADS descriptors, Doppler, ± elastography, and ipsilateral axilla.
- Retroareolar/ductal: radial orientation; distinguish duct ectasia (anechoic, mobile debris, avascular) from intraductal mass (echogenic, vascular).

## Image Optimisation

- Edge shadowing: adjust angle, more gel, compound imaging.
- Side-lobe artefact mimicking cyst echoes: reduce gain, use THI/compound.
- Posterior acoustic features can be altered by compound imaging — briefly disable if equivocal.

## Measurements and Annotation

- Lesion size in **three orthogonal dimensions**.
- Location: clockface, distance from nipple (cm), depth (thirds or cm from skin).
- Orientation: parallel vs non-parallel.
- Axillary nodes: short-axis (SAD), long-axis (LAD), L/S ratio, maximal cortical thickness, hilar status.

{% tab chapter-3 Ultrasound Features %}

## BI-RADS Ultrasound Lexicon

| Descriptor | Categories |
|---|---|
| Shape | Oval, round, irregular |
| Orientation | Parallel, not parallel (taller-than-wide) |
| Margin | Circumscribed; not circumscribed (indistinct, angular, microlobulated, spiculated) |
| Echo pattern | Anechoic, hyperechoic, hypoechoic, isoechoic, complex cystic and solid, heterogeneous |
| Posterior features | None, enhancement, shadowing, combined |
| Associated features | Duct changes, architectural distortion, skin thickening/retraction, oedema, adenopathy, vascularity |

## BI-RADS Categories and Malignancy Risk

- **0:** Incomplete — additional imaging or priors needed
- **1:** Negative — routine care
- **2:** Benign (simple cyst, intramammary node)
- **3:** Probably benign — <<≤2% malignancy risk; short-interval follow-up at 6, 12, and 24 months>>
- **4A / 4B / 4C:** <<>2–10% / >10–50% / >50–95% — biopsy recommended>>
- **5:** <<>95% malignancy risk — treat as cancer until proven otherwise>>
- **6:** Known biopsy-proven malignancy

## Characterising Common Findings

### Simple Cyst (BI-RADS 2)
- Anechoic, thin wall, circumscribed, posterior enhancement, **no internal vascularity**.
- Aspirate only if symptomatic or diagnostically uncertain.

### Complicated Cyst
- Low-level internal echoes, fluid–debris levels, no solid component, no vascularity.
- Typically BI-RADS 2 if classic and asymptomatic; otherwise BI-RADS 3 or aspirate.

### Complex Cystic and Solid Mass (BI-RADS 4)
- Mural nodule, thick septations, vascularity in solid parts — **biopsy required**.

### Fibroadenoma (Typical BI-RADS 3)
- Oval, circumscribed, parallel, homogeneous hypoechoic with gentle lobulations and thin echogenic pseudocapsule.
- Biopsy if atypical, growing (<<>20% in any dimension over 6 months>>), in older patients (>30 with new lesion), or patient preference.

### Suspicious Solid Mass (BI-RADS 4/5)
- Irregular, non-parallel, non-circumscribed (angular/spiculated), heterogeneous hypoechoic, posterior shadowing, echogenic halo, internal vascularity.

### Mastitis / Abscess
- Mastitis: ill-defined hypoechoic area with hyperaemia.
- Abscess: complex cystic with thick wall, peripheral hyperaemia, possible gas.
- Consider **inflammatory carcinoma** if non-resolving or atypical.

### Fat Necrosis, Male Breast, Pregnancy/Lactation, Implants
- Fat necrosis: variable; oil cyst with rim calcifications is characteristic.
- Gynaecomastia: subareolar fan-shaped hypoechoic tissue; suspicious masses are eccentric and vascular.
- Pregnancy: galactocele shows fat–fluid level; ultrasound is first-line.
- Implants: <<stepladder sign indicates intracapsular rupture; snowstorm appearance indicates extracapsular silicone; MRI remains the gold standard>>.

## Axillary Lymph Node Criteria

| Feature | Reassuring | Suspicious |
|---|---|---|
| Shape | Oval (L/S >2) | Round (L/S ≤2) |
| Cortex | <<Thin, uniform ≤2–3 mm>> | <<≥3 mm; highly suspicious if focal bulge or >5 mm>> |
| Hilum | Preserved fatty hilum | <<Effaced or replaced>> |
| Vascularity | Central/hilar | Peripheral/cortical |
| Margin | Smooth | Irregular, matted, perinodal oedema |

The **most specific** feature is a focal cortical bulge with loss of fatty hilum — target this for biopsy.

{% tab chapter-3 Guided Procedures %}

## General Principles

- Consent, time-out, aseptic technique.
- Plan shortest, safest path; **keep needle parallel to chest wall** to minimise pneumothorax risk.
- Real-time, **in-plane** visualisation of the entire needle.
- Local anaesthetic: 1% lidocaine to skin and along the track to the lesion capsule.

## Core Needle Biopsy (CNB)

- Devices: <<spring-loaded 14G core for most solid lesions; vacuum-assisted biopsy (7–10G) for papillary lesions, radial scars, and small/visible calcified masses>>.
- Steps:
  1. Plan entry avoiding skin, nipple, and vessels (use Doppler).
  2. Anaesthetise; small skin nick.
  3. Advance in-plane to just beyond the far margin; confirm in orthogonal view.
  4. Fire with aperture centred in target; obtain **3–5 cores** from different parts of the lesion.
  5. Deploy marker clip if neoadjuvant chemotherapy planned or small lesion.
  6. Compression for haemostasis; steri-strips and dressing.
- Document sample count, clip deployment, complications, BI-RADS, and concordance plan.

## Cyst Aspiration

- Indications: symptomatic, tense, uncertain diagnosis.
- 18–21G needle; full aspiration.
- **If fluid is bloody or a residual mass remains**, send cytology and proceed to CNB.
- Post-aspiration scan to confirm cavity collapse.

## Abscess Drainage

- Large-bore needle or small catheter; break loculations.
- Send pus for culture; coordinate antibiotics and surgical review.

## Axillary Node Sampling

- US-guided core (14G) preferred for histology; FNA per local protocol.
- Target **focal cortical bulge or thickest cortex**; avoid hilum.

## Complications and Mitigation

| Complication | Avoidance |
|---|---|
| Bleeding / haematoma | Doppler to avoid vessels; firm compression post-procedure |
| Infection | Strict asepsis |
| Pneumothorax | Needle parallel to chest wall; avoid deep overshoot |
| Clip migration | Gentle compression; avoid large cavities |
| Sampling error | Traverse solid component; multiple cores; correlate histology |

## Reporting and MDT

- Indication, technique, findings (BI-RADS lexicon, 3D size, location, axilla).
- Impression: **BI-RADS category 0–6**.
- Triple assessment concordance is essential:
  - Concordant benign: discharge or short follow-up.
  - **Discordant** benign pathology with suspicious imaging: repeat biopsy/VAB and MDT discussion.
  - Malignancy: axillary staging, pre-op planning, neoadjuvant consideration.

## Key Take-Aways

- Use a high-frequency linear probe (12–18 MHz) with THI and compound imaging; scan systematically in radial and anti-radial planes.
- BI-RADS 3 lesions carry ≤2% malignancy risk and warrant follow-up at 6, 12, and 24 months; BI-RADS 4 and 5 require biopsy.
- Axillary cortical thickness ≥3 mm, focal bulge, or loss of fatty hilum should prompt nodal sampling — target the most abnormal feature.
- Always assess simple cysts as anechoic, thin-walled, avascular lesions with posterior enhancement; complex cystic and solid masses with vascular solid components require biopsy.
- Keep the biopsy needle parallel to the chest wall, use in-plane visualisation, and document concordance between imaging and pathology at the MDT.
- Elastography and Doppler are supportive adjuncts only and should never override clear grey-scale or clinical features.
