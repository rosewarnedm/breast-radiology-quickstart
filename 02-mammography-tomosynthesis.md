Mammography remains the cornerstone of breast imaging for both screening and symptomatic assessment, with digital breast tomosynthesis (DBT) increasingly supplementing standard 2D acquisitions. This chapter covers the physics, positioning, interpretation framework and decision-making required to deliver high-quality mammographic services. The emphasis is on technical adequacy, the BI-RADS lexicon and integration of findings into the triple assessment pathway.

{% tab chapter-2 Equipment and Physics %}

## Full-Field Digital Mammography (FFDM)

- X-ray energies typically <<25–32 kVp>>, with target/filter combinations (Mo/Mo, Mo/Rh, Rh/Rh, W/Rh) tailored by automatic exposure control (AEC) to breast thickness and composition.
- Anti-scatter grid improves contrast on standard views; magnification views use an air gap **without** a grid.
- Magnification: typically <<1.5×>> with a small focal spot (~0.1 mm).

## Compression

- Typical compression force <<10–18 kg (100–180 N)>>; avoid forces >20 kg where possible.
- Benefits of adequate compression:
  - Reduced scatter and improved contrast
  - Lower glandular dose
  - Decreased motion blur
  - Equalised tissue thickness spreading overlapping structures

## Dose

| Parameter | Typical value |
|---|---|
| Average glandular dose (AGD) per 2D view | <<1.2–2.5 mGy>> |
| Effective dose for 4-view 2D study | <<~0.4 mSv>> |
| DBT per view (vs 2D) | Similar to ~1.2–2× a single 2D view |

## Digital Breast Tomosynthesis (DBT)

- Limited-angle acquisition of multiple low-dose projections, reconstructed into thin slices (typically <<1 mm>>).
- Sweep angle is vendor-dependent (<<approx. 15–50°>>) and affects in-plane resolution and artefacts.
- **Synthetic 2D**: reconstructed from the DBT dataset, avoiding additional 2D exposure and keeping total dose close to a 2D-only exam.

## Key Advantages and Limitations of DBT

- Advantages: improved cancer detection (especially for spiculated masses/architectural distortion), reduced false positives from tissue overlap.
- Limitations:
  - Microcalcification morphology may be less conspicuous than on magnification 2D — **magnification views remain standard for calcifications**.
  - Susceptible to motion and reconstruction artefacts (stair-step, streaks from clips).

{% tab chapter-2 Technique and Positioning %}

## Indications

- **Screening (NHSBSP)**: routine ages <<50–70 years>> (phased extension to 47–73 in some regions).
- **Symptomatic assessment**: palpable lump, suspicious nipple discharge, skin/nipple changes, focal persistent abnormality.
- **Surveillance**: post-treatment follow-up per local protocols.
- **Pre-operative**: disease extent, multifocality, wire/seed localisation.
- **Guided biopsy**: stereotactic or DBT-guided for calcifications and architectural distortion.

## Contraindications and Precautions

- No absolute contraindications.
- **Pregnancy**: screening deferred; diagnostic mammography permitted if clinically indicated with abdominal shielding.
- **Lactation**: safe; advise feeding/pumping immediately before imaging to reduce density.
- **Implants**: use Eklund (implant-displaced) views in addition to standard views where possible.

## Patient Preparation

- Remove deodorants, talc and jewellery (mimic calcifications).
- Apply skin markers for palpable lumps, scars, moles, retracted nipples and cutaneous lesions.
- Confirm labelling: patient ID, side, view, date/time and technologist ID on every image.

## Standard Views

- **CC** (craniocaudal) and **MLO** (mediolateral oblique) for each breast.
- MLO tube angle <<approximately 45–60°>>, tailored to body habitus.

### Adequacy Criteria

| View | Key criteria |
|---|---|
| CC | <<Posterior nipple line within ≤1 cm of MLO PNL>>; medial and posterior tissue included; nipple in profile if possible; no skin folds |
| MLO | <<Pectoralis muscle visible to or below the level of the nipple>>; convex anterior pectoral border; IMF open; adequate superior/posterior tissue |

NHSBSP **PGMI** scoring: aim for **P+G ≥75%** and **Inadequate ≤3%**.

## Problem-Solving Views

- **True lateral (ML/LM)**: triangulation; superior/inferior localisation.
- **XCCL/XCCM**: improve lateral or medial coverage.
- **Cleavage view**: lesions near the midline.
- **Rolled CC (medial/lateral)**: separates overlapping tissue.
- **Spot compression**: resolves summation, defines margins.
- **Magnification views**: 1.5×, small focal spot, no grid — for calcifications and subtle distortion.
- **Eklund views**: in patients with implants.

## Triangulation Tip

- On true lateral compared with MLO: if a lesion moves **superior on ML**, it lies **medial**; if it moves **inferior on ML**, it lies **lateral** ("SAME — Superior Above, Medial Equals").

{% tab chapter-2 Image Interpretation %}

## A Systematic Approach

1. Confirm patient details, side, view and prior comparison.
2. Assess technical adequacy and positioning.
3. Global overview: density, symmetry, post-surgical/radiation change.
4. Focused search: masses, calcifications, asymmetries, architectural distortion, associated features.
5. Correlate CC and MLO; triangulate.
6. Decide on further imaging (spot, magnification, DBT, ultrasound).
7. Assign BI-RADS category with management recommendation.

## Breast Density (BI-RADS a–d)

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| a | Almost entirely fatty |
| b | Scattered fibroglandular density |
| c | Heterogeneously dense — may obscure small masses |
| d | Extremely dense — lowers sensitivity and is an independent risk factor |

## BI-RADS Descriptors

- **Mass**: shape (round/oval/irregular); margin (circumscribed/obscured/microlobulated/indistinct/spiculated); density (high/equal/low/fat-containing).
- **Calcifications — morphology**:
  - **Typically benign**: skin, vascular, coarse/popcorn, rim/eggshell, dystrophic, milk of calcium (teacup on ML), suture, round/punctate.
  - **Suspicious**: amorphous, coarse heterogeneous (~0.5–1 mm), fine pleomorphic (<0.5 mm), fine linear or fine-linear branching ("casting").
- **Calcifications — distribution**: diffuse/scattered, regional, grouped, linear, segmental.
  - Practical rule for "grouped": <<≥5 calcifications within 1 cm or occupying ≤2 cm>>.
- **Asymmetries**: focal, global, **developing** (new, larger or denser than prior — suspicious).
- **Architectural distortion**: spiculations/lines without a discrete mass.
- **Associated features**: skin thickening, nipple retraction, trabecular thickening, oedema, axillary adenopathy.

## BI-RADS Categories and Management

| Category | Meaning | Management |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Incomplete | Prior or additional imaging |
| 1 | Negative | Routine screening |
| 2 | Benign | Routine screening |
| 3 | Probably benign | <<≤2% malignancy risk; short-interval follow-up at 6, 12 and 24 months>> |
| 4 | Suspicious (4A/4B/4C) | <<Image-guided biopsy>> |
| 5 | Highly suggestive of malignancy | <<≥95% malignancy risk; biopsy and appropriate action>> |
| 6 | Biopsy-proven malignancy | Ensure definitive management |

BI-RADS 3 is appropriate only **after** complete diagnostic work-up for: a solitary group of round/punctate calcifications; a noncalcified, circumscribed, oval mass; or focal asymmetry without suspicious features.

## Tomosynthesis Reading

- Scroll sequentially from skin to chest wall; reverse to confirm.
- Use slab/cine views for overview; revert to 1 mm slices for margin and distortion assessment.
- Correlate with 2D or synthetic 2D for calcifications and global context.
- Confirm findings on the orthogonal view; consider rolled or additional tomo view if not seen.
- Watch for DBT artefacts: out-of-plane blur, stair-step (motion), streaks adjacent to clips/markers.

## Common Pitfalls

- Deodorant or talc mimicking calcifications.
- Mistaking summation for a mass — use spot compression and DBT.
- Overcalling benign calcification morphologies — use magnification views.
- Failing to recognise developing asymmetry — always compare with priors.
- Assuming all spiculation is scar — new or progressive spiculation is suspicious.
- Missing axillary tail, subareolar and posterior lesions — use deliberate search patterns and XCCL.

## Special Situations

- **Implants**: standard + Eklund views; MRI is the gold standard for suspected rupture.
- **Post-operative/post-radiation breast**: expected skin thickening and trabecular oedema peak around <<6–12 months post-radiotherapy>>; new findings beyond expected evolution warrant assessment.
- **Male breast**: gynaecomastia patterns (nodular, dendritic, diffuse); eccentric mass, spiculation or suspicious calcifications require ultrasound and biopsy.
- **Pregnancy/lactation**: ultrasound first-line for palpable findings; mammography permissible when indicated.

## Key Take-Aways

- Technical adequacy is the foundation of mammographic interpretation: the <<CC PNL should lie within ≤1 cm of the MLO PNL>> and the pectoralis should reach the nipple level on MLO.
- Use the BI-RADS lexicon systematically for masses, calcifications, asymmetries and architectural distortion, and always compare with priors to detect developing asymmetry.
- DBT improves detection of spiculated masses and architectural distortion but does **not** replace magnification views for calcification assessment.
- BI-RADS 3 carries a <<≤2% malignancy risk>> and is appropriate only after complete diagnostic work-up, with follow-up at 6, 12 and 24 months.
- Keep dose ALARA: adequate compression, correct AEC, and synthetic 2D when performing combined 2D+DBT.
- Integrate imaging findings into triple assessment and ensure radiological–pathological concordance after any image-guided biopsy.
