Masses and architectural distortion (AD) are among the most clinically significant mammographic findings, frequently representing invasive carcinoma. This chapter covers BI-RADS descriptors, stepwise work-up of screen-detected and symptomatic lesions, targeted ultrasound technique, and biopsy decision-making with attention to common pitfalls.

{% tab chapter-8 Mass Descriptors %}

## Definition

A **mass** (mammography) is a three-dimensional space-occupying lesion seen on **two different projections** with a convex-outward margin. A finding seen on only one view is a *focal asymmetry* or possible summation until proven otherwise.

## Mammographic Descriptors (BI-RADS)

| Feature | Categories |
|---|---|
| **Shape** | Round, oval, irregular |
| **Margin** | Circumscribed, obscured, microlobulated, indistinct, spiculated |
| **Density** | High, equal, low, fat-containing |

- <<Fat-containing masses (oil cyst, lipoma, galactocele, hamartoma) are BI-RADS 2 — virtually always benign.>>
- <<Spiculated margin is the single mammographic descriptor most predictive of malignancy.>>
- High density relative to fibroglandular tissue raises suspicion; low density does not exclude malignancy.

## Ultrasound Descriptors

- **Shape:** oval, round, irregular.
- **Orientation:** parallel (wider-than-tall) vs **non-parallel (taller-than-wide)** — the latter favours malignancy.
- **Margin:** circumscribed, indistinct, angular, microlobulated, spiculated.
- **Echo pattern:** anechoic, hypoechoic, isoechoic, hyperechoic, complex cystic and solid, heterogeneous.
- **Posterior features:** enhancement (often benign/cystic), shadowing (suspicious), combined, none.
- **Surrounding tissue:** echogenic halo, duct changes, Cooper's ligament thickening, skin thickening.
- **Vascularity:** internal flow raises suspicion; absent flow does not exclude malignancy.

## Suspicious Combinations

- Mammography/DBT: irregular, spiculated, high-density mass ± AD ± pleomorphic calcifications → BI-RADS 4C–5.
- Ultrasound: irregular, **non-parallel**, spiculated/angular margin, hypoechoic, posterior shadowing, echogenic halo, internal vascularity.

## BI-RADS Categories and Malignancy Likelihood

| Category | Likelihood | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Incomplete | Additional imaging / priors |
| 1 | Negative | Routine |
| 2 | Benign | Routine |
| 3 | <<≤2%>> | <<6-month short-interval follow-up>> |
| 4A | <<2–10%>> | Biopsy |
| 4B | <<10–50%>> | Biopsy |
| 4C | <<50–95%>> | Biopsy |
| 5 | <<≥95%>> | Biopsy/treatment |
| 6 | Known malignancy | Therapy planning |

## Common Benign Masses

- **Simple cyst:** anechoic, thin wall, posterior enhancement, no internal vascularity → BI-RADS 2.
- **Complicated cyst:** low-level internal echoes, no solid component → BI-RADS 3 with 6-month follow-up; aspirate if symptomatic.
- **Complex cystic and solid mass:** vascular solid component or thick nodular septations → <<BI-RADS 4 — biopsy required.>>
- **Fibroadenoma:** oval, parallel, circumscribed, homogeneous hypoechoic; "popcorn" calcification when involuting.
- **Intramammary lymph node:** <<short-axis typically <10 mm with fatty hilum and cortical thickness ≤3 mm — BI-RADS 2 if classic.>>
- **Hamartoma:** "breast within a breast" with fat and soft-tissue elements within a capsule.

{% tab chapter-8 Architectural Distortion %}

## Definition

**Architectural distortion (AD):** disruption of normal parenchymal architecture with lines or spiculations radiating from a point, focal retraction, or tethering, **without a definable mass**. AD is among the imaging features most associated with invasive carcinoma, especially **invasive lobular carcinoma (ILC)** and radial scar/complex sclerosing lesion (CSL).

## Detection and Confirmation

- **DBT increases detection of AD** compared with 2D mammography by reducing tissue overlap; scroll thin (1 mm) slices to identify the **epicentre** and the length of spicules.
- Confirm with **spot compression** ± **rolled views** to exclude summation artefact.
- Compare meticulously with priors — postsurgical scar should be stable or diminish over time and lie along the surgical track.

## Targeted Ultrasound for AD

- Look for subtle hypoechoic shadowing without a discrete mass, focal interruption of tissue planes, thickened/retracted Cooper's ligaments, or a small irregular hypoechoic correlate.
- Negative ultrasound does **not** downgrade suspicious AD.

## Differential Diagnosis

| Cause | Key features |
|---|---|
| Invasive carcinoma (especially ILC) | New AD, no surgical history, may have associated subtle mass/calcifications |
| Radial scar / CSL | Long thin spicules, radiolucent centre, no central mass |
| Postsurgical scar / fat necrosis | Matches incision; stable or diminishing; oil cysts, rim calcifications |
| Summation artefact | Visible on one view only; resolves on spot/rolled views or DBT |

## Step-by-Step Work-up

1. Confirm AD on DBT and additional views (spot compression, rolled).
2. Review surgical/trauma history and prior imaging.
3. Perform targeted ultrasound for sonographic correlate.
4. If no benign explanation → **biopsy**:
   - <<DBT-guided or stereotactic vacuum-assisted biopsy (7–10G) is preferred for pure AD without ultrasound correlate.>>
   - Ultrasound-guided 14G core biopsy if a clear sonographic correlate is identified.
5. Deploy a marker clip and obtain a post-biopsy mammogram to confirm clip position within the AD.
6. Correlate imaging and pathology — discordance mandates further sampling or surgical excision.

## BI-RADS Assignment for AD

- AD without benign explanation: **BI-RADS 4** (A–C depending on features) or **BI-RADS 5** if classic spiculated AD with skin/nipple retraction and/or suspicious calcifications.
- <<AD on DBT with negative ultrasound still warrants biopsy — do not assign BI-RADS 3.>>

## B3 Outcomes after Biopsy

- **Radial scar/CSL:** vacuum-assisted excision (VAE) commonly performed; surgical excision if atypia or inadequate sampling.
- **Papillary lesion without atypia:** VAE.
- **Atypia (ADH, ALH, LCIS) within a B3 lesion:** <<generally requires surgical excision or VAE per local protocol due to upgrade risk.>>

{% tab chapter-8 Asymmetries %}

## BI-RADS Asymmetry Terminology

| Term | Definition | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Asymmetry** | Density on **one view only** | Usually summation; further views to exclude true mass |
| **Focal asymmetry** | Density on **two views** lacking convex borders of a mass | Often benign but requires work-up |
| **Global asymmetry** | Large volume of asymmetric tissue (≥1 quadrant) | Usually normal variant if no associated findings |
| **Developing asymmetry** | New, larger or more conspicuous than prior | <<High suspicion — malignancy rate approximately 12–15%; biopsy usually required>> |

## Distinguishing Mass, Asymmetry and Summation

- **True mass:** convex borders, seen on two views, **persists on spot compression**.
- **Focal asymmetry:** lacks convex borders; may persist but typically lacks a definable centre.
- **Summation artefact:** only seen on one view; **resolves** on rolled views, spot compression, or DBT.

## Work-up Pathway

1. Identify the finding and verify on both views.
2. Perform **spot compression** ± **rolled CC** views; DBT if available.
3. If finding resolves → summation artefact, BI-RADS 1/2.
4. If finding persists:
   - Targeted ultrasound at the corresponding location.
   - If ultrasound correlate suspicious → ultrasound-guided core biopsy.
   - If no ultrasound correlate but developing or suspicious morphology → **stereotactic/DBT-guided biopsy**.

## Practical BI-RADS Examples

- Asymmetry on one view only that resolves on spot compression: **BI-RADS 1**.
- Stable focal asymmetry over multiple prior rounds with fatty content and no architectural change: **BI-RADS 2**.
- New focal asymmetry that persists with no ultrasound correlate but no suspicious features and no priors: **BI-RADS 3** with 6-month follow-up.
- **Developing asymmetry** persisting on spot compression in a 58-year-old, even with negative ultrasound: <<BI-RADS 4 — biopsy (stereotactic/DBT-guided if no sonographic correlate).>>

## Pitfalls

- Calling summation a mass — always use spot compression and DBT.
- Assigning BI-RADS 3 to a developing asymmetry — this finding carries a malignancy rate well above the 2% BI-RADS 3 threshold.
- Negative ultrasound falsely reassuring for ILC presenting as subtle asymmetry/AD.

## Triangulation and Localisation

- Use the MLO view to estimate depth (anterior/middle/posterior third) and CC view to localise medial vs lateral, then convert to clock-face position.
- Record distance from the nipple (mm) and from the skin (mm) for any actionable finding.
- For biopsy planning, document three orthogonal measurements on the modality used for intervention.

## Key Take-Aways

- A mass must be seen on **two views with convex margins**; one-view findings are asymmetries or summation until proven otherwise.
- **Spiculated margin** is the single most predictive mammographic descriptor of malignancy; **non-parallel orientation with posterior shadowing** is the most suspicious ultrasound combination.
- Architectural distortion on DBT without ultrasound correlate **still warrants biopsy** — typically DBT-guided vacuum-assisted — and must not be assigned BI-RADS 3.
- **Developing asymmetry** carries a malignancy rate well above the BI-RADS 3 threshold and should be biopsied even with negative ultrasound.
- Always correlate imaging with pathology; **discordant benign results require repeat sampling** or surgical excision.
- For every actionable lesion, document side, clock-face position, distance from nipple and skin, three orthogonal measurements, BI-RADS category and explicit recommendation.
