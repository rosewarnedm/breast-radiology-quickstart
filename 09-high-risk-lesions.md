High-risk (B3) breast lesions sit between benign and malignant on the histological spectrum and carry both an upgrade risk on excision and an increased future cancer risk. Their management depends on lesion type, presence of atypia, sampling adequacy, and radiology–pathology concordance. This chapter provides a practical, MDT-focused framework for the breast radiology trainee.

{% tab chapter-9 ADH and ALH %}

## Atypical Ductal Hyperplasia (ADH)

### Typical imaging
- Grouped **amorphous** to **fine pleomorphic** calcifications
- Occasionally a small mass or architectural distortion
- BI-RADS category is determined by imaging features; "high-risk" histology does **not** downgrade BI-RADS

### Biopsy approach
- Preferred: stereotactic or DBT-guided **8–9G vacuum-assisted biopsy (VAB)**
- 14G core often inadequate for calcifications — risk of underestimation
- Always perform **specimen radiography** to confirm representative calcifications
- **Place a clip** in all sampled lesions

### Upgrade risk
- <<ADH upgrade to DCIS/invasive carcinoma is approximately 10–30% on excision when concordant and adequately sampled>>
- Higher upgrade if: limited sampling (14G core), residual calcifications, associated mass/distortion, fine pleomorphic morphology

### Management
1. **ADH on 14G core or discordant** → further VAB or surgical diagnostic excision
2. **ADH on VAB, target largely removed, concordant** → vacuum-assisted excision (VAE) or surgical excision per local protocol
3. **Residual calcifications, discordance, or associated mass** → surgical excision with wire/seed localisation

### Future cancer risk
- <<ADH confers a bilateral relative risk of approximately 4–5× the general population>>
- Discuss risk-reduction: chemoprevention (e.g., tamoxifen) in suitable patients, family history/genetics referral if indicated
- Consider annual mammography for 5 years post-excision (local policy)

## Lobular Neoplasia: ALH and Classic LCIS

### Key features
- Often **incidental** finding on cores targeting another abnormality
- E-cadherin **negative** on immunohistochemistry
- Generally not associated with calcifications themselves (calcifications usually in adjacent benign tissue)

### Upgrade risk (concordant, incidental)
- <<Classic LCIS/ALH upgrade to DCIS/invasive carcinoma is 0–5% if incidental and concordant>>
- Higher if the LN is the targeted finding or discordance exists

### Management
- **Incidental + concordant**: no surgical excision; risk counselling and tailored surveillance
- **Targeted finding or discordant**: further sampling with VAB or surgical excision

### Florid LCIS and Pleomorphic LCIS (PLCIS)
- <<Florid LCIS/PLCIS carries an upgrade risk of 20–40% and is managed akin to DCIS>>
- Surgical excision with margin targets — <<commonly ≥2 mm margins for PLCIS, per local DCIS policy>>
- Consider radiotherapy per MDT

### Future cancer risk
- Classic LCIS/ALH: **bilateral** relative risk approximately **2–10×**
- Discuss enhanced screening; MRI screening if calculated lifetime risk exceeds local threshold (<<commonly ≥20–30% by validated risk models>>)

## Intraductal Papilloma

### Typical presentation
- Pathological **single-duct nipple discharge** (often bloody)
- Subareolar intraductal mass on ultrasound
- May present as calcifications on screening

### Imaging features
- **Ultrasound**: intraductal vascular frond-like mass ± duct dilatation; **vascular stalk on Doppler**; parallel orientation
- **MRI**: enhancing intraductal mass, may show internal fronds
- **Galactography/ductography**: filling defect (largely superseded by US/MRI)

### Biopsy approach
- **Ultrasound-guided 10–12G VAB** preferred to maximise diagnostic yield
- 14G core risks underestimation — avoid if VAB available
- Clip placement essential

### Upgrade risk
- <<Papillary lesion without atypia: upgrade risk 2–7%>>
- <<Papillary lesion with atypia: upgrade risk 10–30%>>

### Management

| Finding | Recommended action |
|---|---|
| Papilloma **without** atypia, concordant, completely removed on VAB | VAE to complete excision preferred; surveillance acceptable if fully removed |
| Papilloma **without** atypia, partial sampling | VAE or surgical excision |
| Papilloma **with** atypia | **Surgical excision** |
| Papillary **carcinoma** | Manage as carcinoma per MDT |

### Practical tips
- For subareolar lesions, mark the duct's relationship to the nipple to guide surgery
- Symptomatic discharge usually resolves after excision

## Radial Scar / Complex Sclerosing Lesion (CSL)

### Imaging
- **Architectural distortion** with central radiolucency ("black star")
- **Long thin spicules** without a discrete central mass on mammography/DBT
- Ultrasound: hypoechoic area with posterior shadowing
- MRI: variable — can mimic carcinoma with rapid enhancement and washout

### Definitions
- **Radial scar**: <10 mm
- <<Complex sclerosing lesion (CSL): ≥10 mm>>

### Biopsy
- **DBT/stereotactic-guided 8–9G VAB** with bracketing around the locus of distortion
- 14G cores often inadequate

### Management
- **Without atypia, ≤20 mm, adequately sampled, concordant**: VAE or imaging surveillance
- <<With atypia, lesion >20 mm, or discordance: surgical excision is recommended>>

---

## Flat Epithelial Atypia (FEA) / Columnar Cell Lesions

- Imaging: grouped **amorphous** calcifications
- <<Isolated FEA upgrade risk: approximately 2–10% when concordant and adequately sampled>>

**Management:**
- Isolated FEA on adequate VAB, calcifications removed, concordant → **surveillance** (e.g., annual mammography × 5 years)
- 14G core only, residual calcifications, or discordance → VAE; surgical excision if atypia persists

---

## Mucocele-Like Lesion (MLL)

- Imaging: grouped calcifications; ultrasound shows **clustered microcysts with echogenic mucin**
- Without atypia: upgrade ~0–5%
- <<With atypia: upgrade risk up to ~20%, with association with mucinous carcinoma>>

**Management:**
- Without atypia, concordant: VAE or surveillance after adequate sampling
- With atypia or discordant: **surgical excision**

---

## Fibroepithelial Lesion (FEL) — Possible Phyllodes

### Features suggesting phyllodes on core/imaging
- <<Size >30 mm, rapid growth, age >40, heterogeneous echotexture with clefts, or cellular stroma/stromal atypia on core>>

### Management
- Surgical excision recommended
- <<For borderline/malignant phyllodes, aim for ≥1 cm clear margins>>
- Small, benign-leaning FELs may undergo VAE with surveillance if concordant

---

## Atypical Apocrine Lesions

- Often present as calcifications
- Many units recommend **VAE or excision** given underestimation risk — discuss at MDT

---

## Radiology–Pathology Concordance Checklist

Ask three questions at MDT:
1. Was the **correct target** sampled? (Clip position, specimen radiograph)
2. Does the histology **plausibly explain** the imaging?
3. Was sampling method/volume **adequate**? (VAB for calcifications/distortion preferred)

- **Concordant + adequate** → lesion-specific management (VAE vs surveillance)
- **Discordant or inadequate** → repeat VAB or surgical diagnostic excision

## Key Take-Aways

- B3 lesions are **not cancers**, but each requires an active plan: exclude upgrade and address future risk.
- Use the right tool: <<8–9G VAB for calcifications and distortion; 10–12G VAB for ultrasound-visible papillary lesions>>; always place a clip and confirm calcifications on specimen radiography.
- Radiology–pathology **concordance** must be assessed at MDT for every B3 result; discordance mandates further sampling.
- Lesion-specific management: ADH and atypia-containing lesions usually escalate to VAE or surgical excision; isolated concordant FEA or incidental classic LN may be observed after adequate sampling.
- Counsel patients on **bilateral** increased risk for ADH and LN; consider enhanced surveillance and chemoprevention where appropriate.
- Florid LCIS and PLCIS behave clinically like DCIS — manage with surgical excision and appropriate margins per local DCIS policy.
