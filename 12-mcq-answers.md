{% tab chapter-12 Introduction and Techniques %}

## Chapter 1 — Introduction to Breast Imaging

- **Q1: B** — Ultrasound is first-line in symptomatic women under 35 because of denser breast tissue and to <<avoid ionising radiation in younger patients>>.
- **Q2: D** — Triple assessment requires <<concordance of clinical examination, imaging, and needle biopsy>>; discordance mandates further work-up.
- **Q3: C** — A hard, irregular, tethered lump requires <<urgent two-week-wait referral>>.
- **Q4: A** — Generalised cyclical nodularity with normal imaging is managed by reassurance and routine follow-up.
- **Q5: E** — <<BI-RADS 0 indicates an incomplete assessment requiring additional imaging>>.

## Chapter 2 — Mammographic Technique and Physics

- **Q1: A** — Adequate compression improves image quality, reduces motion blur, and lowers dose.
- **Q2: E** — An adequate MLO must include the <<inframammary fold and pectoralis muscle to or below the level of the posterior nipple line>>.
- **Q3: C** — Average glandular dose for a standard digital mammographic view is approximately <<1–2 mGy per view>>.
- **Q4: B** — Anti-scatter grids improve contrast by reducing scatter but increase dose.
- **Q5: D** — Anode/filter combinations (Mo/Mo, Rh/Rh, W/Rh) are matched to breast thickness and composition to optimise the X-ray spectrum.

## Chapter 3 — Mammographic Interpretation and BI-RADS

- **Q1: C** — <<BI-RADS 3 carries <2% malignancy risk>> and is typically followed at 6, 12, and 24 months.
- **Q2: A** — Spiculated margins are among the strongest mammographic predictors of malignancy.
- **Q3: E** — Architectural distortion without a definite mass warrants DBT and targeted ultrasound; persistent suspicious distortion should be biopsied.
- **Q4: B** — <<Fine linear or fine-linear branching calcifications in a segmental distribution are highly suspicious (BI-RADS 4C–5)>>.
- **Q5: D** — Stable global asymmetry without focal features is usually benign; a developing asymmetry requires work-up.

## Chapter 4 — Breast Ultrasound

- **Q1: A** — Posterior acoustic shadowing may be seen in both malignancy and benign fibrous tissue; assessment relies on combined features.
- **Q2: D** — Circumscribed, oval, parallel ("wider-than-tall") masses with homogeneous echotexture favour benignity.
- **Q3: C** — Suspicious axillary nodes show <<cortical thickness ≥3 mm, focal cortical bulge, or loss of fatty hilum>>.
- **Q4: B** — Elastography improves specificity, but benign entities such as scar tissue can also appear stiff.
- **Q5: E** — Doppler aids characterisation but <<absence of internal vascularity does not exclude malignancy>>.

## Chapter 5 — Breast MRI

- **Q1: C** — Indications include high-risk screening, problem solving, extent-of-disease mapping, and implant assessment.
- **Q2: B** — <<Type III (washout) enhancement kinetics are the most suspicious>>, though morphology remains paramount.
- **Q3: E** — Segmental/ductal non-mass enhancement with clumped internal pattern is suspicious for DCIS.
- **Q4: A** — <<Gadolinium is generally avoided in pregnancy>>; MRI in lactation is safe, with negligible gadolinium excretion in breast milk.
- **Q5: D** — Intracapsular silicone rupture demonstrates the <<"linguine sign" on MRI>>.

## Chapter 6 — Breast Screening

- **Q1: A** — The NHS Breast Screening Programme invites women aged <<50–70 years for 3-yearly screening>>.
- **Q2: D** — Key metrics include cancer detection rate, recall rate, PPV of recall, interval cancer rate, and proportion of small/low-stage cancers.
- **Q3: B** — DBT reduces recall for overlapping tissue and increases invasive cancer detection.
- **Q4: E** — <<Double reading with arbitration>> improves sensitivity while maintaining acceptable recall.
- **Q5: C** — Incident rounds have lower recall and higher PPV than prevalent (first) screens.

## Chapter 7 — Microcalcifications

- **Q1: B** — Amorphous calcifications are <<BI-RADS 4A (indeterminate)>> and generally biopsied if new or grouped.
- **Q2: E** — Fine pleomorphic or fine-linear/branching segmental calcifications are <<BI-RADS 4C–5>>.
- **Q3: C** — Secretory rod-like calcifications are usually benign; new segmental amorphous ductal calcifications are suspicious.
- **Q4: A** — <<Stereotactic vacuum-assisted biopsy is the technique of choice for calcifications>>, with at least 12 large-core samples and specimen radiography.
- **Q5: D** — Milk of calcium <<layers ("teacup") on true lateral views>>, supporting benignity.

## Chapter 8 — Masses, Asymmetry and Distortion

- **Q1: A** — Architectural distortion is often occult on 2D but conspicuous on DBT; targeted ultrasound increases correlate yield.
- **Q2: C** — Developing asymmetry warrants work-up; <<stability over two years>> supports benignity.
- **Q3: E** — A spiculated mass with desmoplastic reaction and tethering is BI-RADS 5.
- **Q4: B** — Complex cystic and solid masses require biopsy, not aspiration alone.
- **Q5: D** — Radial scars warrant <<vacuum-assisted excision biopsy>> because of upgrade risk and imaging mimicry of carcinoma.

## Chapter 9 — Biopsy and Localisation

- **Q1: C** — <<14G core biopsy is standard for solid masses>>; FNA has lower specificity for histological subtyping.
- **Q2: A** — Clip placement after vacuum biopsy or excision of calcifications marks the site for future correlation.
- **Q3: D** — Preoperative localisation may use wire, seed, or magnetic markers per local protocol and MDT preference.
- **Q4: B** — B3 lesions (e.g. ADH, radial scar) are typically managed with vacuum-assisted excision for diagnostic and therapeutic sampling.
- **Q5: E** — Antiplatelets/anticoagulants increase bleeding risk; most percutaneous biopsies proceed with careful planning per local protocol.

## Chapter 10 — Special Populations and Implants

- **Q1: A** — In pregnancy/lactation, <<ultrasound is first-line>>; mammography is permissible with shielding; <<avoid gadolinium MRI in pregnancy>>.
- **Q2: D** — Gynaecomastia shows flame-shaped retroareolar tissue; male breast cancer is typically eccentric to the nipple with spiculation or calcifications.
- **Q3: B** — <<Eklund (implant-displaced) views>> are used to visualise native tissue in patients with implants.
- **Q4: E** — <<BIA-ALCL typically presents as a delayed peri-implant effusion or mass, most often with textured implants>>; ultrasound-guided aspiration with CD30 immunohistochemistry is key.
- **Q5: C** — <<MRI is most sensitive and specific for intracapsular silicone rupture>>; ultrasound may show the "stepladder" sign.

## Chapter 11 — Staging and Post-treatment Imaging

- **Q1: C** — Suspicious axillary nodes: <<cortical thickness ≥3 mm, eccentric/focal bulge, loss of fatty hilum>>; biopsy is indicated.
- **Q2: A** — DCIS without invasion is staged <<Tis>>; management is surgical ± radiotherapy, with MRI to define extent in selected cases.
- **Q3: E** — MDT discussion integrates radiology, pathology, and clinical findings; discordance mandates re-biopsy or further imaging.
- **Q4: B** — Post-surgical seromas appear as anechoic/complex fluid cavities; fat necrosis may calcify and mimic malignancy — clinical correlation is essential.
- **Q5: D** — <<MRI is the most accurate modality for assessing neoadjuvant response>>; ultrasound assesses nodal response and mammography monitors calcific DCIS.
