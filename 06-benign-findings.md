Benign and probably benign findings dominate everyday breast imaging, and accurate categorisation prevents both unnecessary biopsies and missed cancers. This chapter consolidates BI-RADS lexicon, classic benign entities, and the structured management of probably benign disease.

{% tab chapter-6 BI-RADS 1 to 3 %}

## Definitions and Risk Thresholds

| Category | Meaning | Malignancy Risk | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| BI-RADS 1 | Negative | Essentially 0% | Routine screening |
| BI-RADS 2 | Benign | Essentially 0% | Routine screening |
| BI-RADS 3 | Probably benign | <<≤2%>> | <<Short-interval follow-up at 6, 12 and 24 months>> |

## Prerequisites for BI-RADS 3

- Only assigned **after a complete diagnostic work-up** — never from screening alone.
- Strict morphological criteria must be met, with **no suspicious features**.
- Patient must be suitable and reliable for follow-up.

## BI-RADS 3 Criteria by Modality

**Mammography:**
- Non-calcified oval, circumscribed, equal/low-density mass
- Solitary group of round or punctate calcifications
- Focal asymmetry without suspicious features

**Ultrasound:**
- Solid oval, circumscribed, parallel (wider-than-tall) mass with gentle lobulations (≤3)
- Complicated cyst (low-level echoes, no solid component, no flow)
- Clustered microcysts
- Multiple bilateral circumscribed masses

**MRI (use sparingly):**
- Small focus or circumscribed oval mass, benign morphology, persistent enhancement, no diffusion restriction

## When NOT to Use BI-RADS 3

- Any suspicious features (spiculation, distortion, fine linear/branching calcifications, non-parallel orientation, washout kinetics)
- **Developing asymmetry** — always BI-RADS 4
- Concurrent ipsilateral cancer or pre-operative staging
- High-risk genetic background where threshold for biopsy is lower
- Pathological nipple discharge (bloody, unilateral, single-duct)
- Patient unable or unlikely to comply with follow-up

## Upgrade Triggers

- <<Interval growth >20% in maximum diameter over 6 months>>
- Any new suspicious morphological feature
- Patient preference or anxiety
- Concern about adherence to follow-up

## Classic Mammographic Benign Patterns

**Fat-containing lesions (always BI-RADS 2):**
- **Oil cyst / fat necrosis:** radiolucent mass ± fat–fluid level; rim ("eggshell") calcification over time
- **Lipoma:** radiolucent, encapsulated, compressible
- **Hamartoma (fibroadenolipoma):** "breast within a breast" — encapsulated mix of fat and soft tissue

**Benign calcifications:**

| Type | Appearance | Confirmation |
|---|---|---|
| Skin (dermal) | Lucent-centred, clustered | Tangential view ± skin marker |
| Vascular | Tram-line parallel tracks | Follow vessel path |
| Milk of calcium | Smudgy on CC, "teacup" layering on lateral | <<True lateral magnified view>> |
| Dystrophic | Coarse >0.5 mm, irregular | History of trauma/surgery/radiotherapy |
| Secretory | Coarse rod-like, radiating to nipple | Often bilateral |
| Popcorn | Large, coarse, central | Involuting fibroadenoma |

## Ultrasound Benign Entities

**Simple cyst** — all criteria must be present:
- Anechoic
- Thin, imperceptible wall
- Circumscribed
- Posterior acoustic enhancement
- **No internal vascularity**

**Intramammary lymph node:**
- Oval/reniform with echogenic fatty hilum
- <<Cortex ≤3 mm>>
- Hilar (not peripheral) vascularity
- Loss of hilum or cortical bulge → suspicious

**Fibroadenoma (typical):**
- Oval, circumscribed, parallel orientation
- Gentle lobulations (≤3)
- Homogeneous, minimal vascularity
- BI-RADS 3 if new; biopsy if >3 cm, growing, atypical, or patient >35–40

**Galactocele:** lactating/postpartum patient; fat–fluid level — BI-RADS 2 if classic.

## MRI Benign Features

- Classic fat-containing lesions: India-ink (out-of-phase) signal drop
- Simple cyst: T2 bright, no enhancement, no diffusion restriction
- <<ADC values >1.3 × 10⁻³ mm²/s support benignity>> (interpret with morphology and kinetics)
- Postsurgical scar: thin enhancement early; low T2 fibrous band late
- Persistent (Type 1) enhancement curve favours benignity

## Distinguishing Look-Alikes

| Lesion | Mimic | Differentiator |
|---|---|---|
| Complicated cyst | Complex cystic & solid mass | <<Any solid component or internal vascularity → BI-RADS 4>> |
| Dermal calcifications | Parenchymal calcifications | Tangential view + skin marker |
| Vascular calcifications | Fine linear branching | Tram-line pattern along vessel |
| Postsurgical scar | Recurrence | Stable/regressing over time; correlate clinically |
| Early fat necrosis | Malignancy | History of trauma/surgery; evolution to rim calcification |

## Step-by-Step Diagnostic Workflow

**1. Complete the diagnostic work-up before categorising**

- **Mammography:** spot compression, magnification (typically 1.5×), true lateral (ML/LM) for calcification layering, tangential views with skin markers if dermal.
- **Ultrasound:** high-frequency linear probe (12–18 MHz), orthogonal planes, three-dimensional measurements, clock-face position and distance from nipple, colour/power Doppler with low PRF.
- **MRI:** assess morphology first, then kinetics, then DWI/ADC.

**2. Apply BI-RADS criteria strictly**

- BI-RADS 2 if classic benign features are present.
- BI-RADS 3 only if **all** suspicious features are excluded and patient is suitable for follow-up.
- Otherwise → BI-RADS 4/5.

**3. Structured follow-up of BI-RADS 3**

| Time point | Action |
|---|---|
| 6 months | Targeted imaging (same modality), unilateral if appropriate |
| 12 months | Bilateral imaging |
| 24 months | Bilateral imaging — if stable, downgrade to BI-RADS 2 |

**4. Upgrade promptly if:**
- <<>20% growth in maximum diameter at 6 months>>
- New suspicious morphology
- Patient preference or unreliable follow-up

## Decision-Making Pearls

**Symptomatic young woman with typical fibroadenoma on ultrasound:**
- BI-RADS 3 acceptable if criteria met and follow-up assured
- Lower threshold for biopsy if >3 cm, growing, or patient anxious

**Pathological nipple discharge:**
- Avoid BI-RADS 3 — pursue tissue diagnosis even if imaging suggests papilloma

**High-risk patient (BRCA, strong family history):**
- Conservative use of BI-RADS 3; favour biopsy or closer MRI surveillance after MDM discussion

**Pre-operative staging in known cancer:**
- Avoid BI-RADS 3 — definitive characterisation required

**Pregnancy and lactation:**
- Ultrasound first-line; <<avoid gadolinium-enhanced MRI in pregnancy>>; mammography permissible with abdominal shielding if needed

**Implants:**
- Use implant-displaced views; capsular calcification and fat necrosis are benign; any new solid mass requires careful assessment

## Reporting Template for BI-RADS 3

A robust BI-RADS 3 report should include:
- Clock-face location and distance from nipple
- Exact measurements in three planes with comparison to priors
- All BI-RADS lexicon descriptors used
- Explicit category with clear management plan and time interval
- Stated upgrade criteria

**Example:**
*"9 × 6 × 5 mm oval, parallel, circumscribed hypoechoic mass with gentle lobulations and no internal vascularity at right 10 o'clock, 4 cm from nipple. Probably benign (BI-RADS 3). Recommend ultrasound follow-up at 6, 12 and 24 months. Biopsy if interval growth or new suspicious features."*

## Common Pitfalls

- Labelling amorphous or coarse heterogeneous calcifications as BI-RADS 3 — these are <<BI-RADS 4>>
- Assigning BI-RADS 3 from screening without diagnostic work-up
- Calling complex cystic and solid masses "complicated cysts"
- Missing milk of calcium by not obtaining a true lateral magnified view
- Underestimating subtle interval growth — measure consistently in identical planes
- Using BI-RADS 3 in high-risk contexts or in pregnancy follow-up

## Key Take-Aways

- BI-RADS 3 carries a <<≤2% malignancy risk>> and is only valid after complete diagnostic work-up with strict morphological criteria met.
- Standard BI-RADS 3 follow-up is <<imaging at 6, 12 and 24 months>> using the same modality; downgrade to BI-RADS 2 after 2 years of stability.
- <<Growth >20% in maximum diameter over 6 months>> or any new suspicious feature mandates upgrade to biopsy.
- Classic fat-containing lesions, simple cysts, typical intramammary lymph nodes (cortex <<≤3 mm>>) and benign calcification patterns are BI-RADS 2 — not BI-RADS 3.
- Avoid BI-RADS 3 in developing asymmetry, pathological nipple discharge, pre-operative staging, high-risk patients, or where follow-up adherence is uncertain.
- Always obtain true lateral magnified views for calcifications and use tangential views with skin markers to confirm dermal location.
