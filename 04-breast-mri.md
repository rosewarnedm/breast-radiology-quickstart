Breast MRI is the most sensitive modality for detecting invasive breast cancer and assessing disease extent, complementing rather than replacing mammography and ultrasound. This chapter covers when to request MRI, how to acquire and quality-check a study, how to apply the BI-RADS MRI lexicon, and how findings translate into multidisciplinary management.

{% tab chapter-4 Indications and Protocol %}

## Clinical indications

- **Pre-operative staging** in biopsy-proven cancer, especially:
  - Invasive lobular carcinoma (multifocal/multicentric risk)
  - Discrepancy between clinical, sonographic and mammographic size
  - Dense breasts where mammography underestimates disease
- **Neoadjuvant systemic therapy (NAST)**: early functional response and end-of-therapy mapping
- **High-risk screening**:
  - <<Pathogenic variant carriers (BRCA1/2, TP53, PALB2)>>
  - <<Prior chest irradiation between ages 10–30 years>>
  - <<Calculated lifetime risk ≥20–25% (Tyrer–Cuzick/CanRisk)>>
- **Problem-solving**: inconclusive conventional imaging; pathological nipple discharge; occult primary with axillary nodal metastases
- **Implants**: suspected rupture (non-contrast protocol)
- **Post-treatment**: differentiating scar/fat necrosis from recurrence

## Contraindications and precautions

- **MRI-related**: non-conditional cardiac devices/implants, ferromagnetic intra-ocular foreign bodies, certain aneurysm clips, severe claustrophobia, inability to lie prone
- **Gadolinium-related**:
  - <<eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m²: avoid unless benefit clearly outweighs risk; use a macrocyclic agent if proceeding>>
  - <<Pregnancy: avoid gadolinium unless maternal benefit clearly outweighs fetal risk>>
  - <<Lactation: gadolinium is permitted and breastfeeding may continue uninterrupted>>
- **Timing**:
  - <<Premenopausal women: schedule on days 7–14 of the menstrual cycle to minimise BPE>>
  - Avoid within 7–10 days of core biopsy; ideally ≥6–8 weeks post-surgery; ~6–12 months after radiotherapy unless urgent

## Patient preparation

- Safety questionnaire; renal function if contrast planned; pregnancy status
- Large-bore antecubital cannula (18–22G) for power injection
- Remove external metal; no deodorant or cosmetics over chest
- Premedicate for anxiety per departmental protocol

## Equipment

- Dedicated multichannel bilateral breast coil
- 1.5T or 3T; 3T gives higher SNR but more susceptibility — Dixon fat suppression preferred
- Prone positioning with pendant breasts, gentle immobilisation, nipples centred

## Standard diagnostic DCE protocol

| Sequence | Purpose | Key parameters |
|---|---|---|
| Axial T2 (±FS) | Cysts, oedema, ducts | 3–4 mm slice |
| Axial T1 non-FS | Haemorrhage, fat, baseline | 3–4 mm slice |
| Axial DWI + ADC | Diffusion characterisation | <<b-values 0, 50–100, and 800–1000 s/mm²>> |
| Axial 3D T1 FS DCE | Lesion detection/kinetics | <<≤1.0 mm in-plane, ≤1.5 mm slice, ≤90 s temporal resolution>> |
| Subtractions + MIPs | Review/screening | Derived |

- **Contrast**: <<macrocyclic gadolinium 0.1 mmol/kg at 2 mL/s, followed by 15–20 mL saline flush>>
- 1 pre-contrast + at least 4–6 post-contrast phases over ~5–8 minutes

## Protocol variants

- **Abbreviated MRI (Ab-MRI)** for screening: 1 pre + 1 early post-contrast 3D acquisition with subtractions/MIPs ± DWI
- **Implant integrity protocol** (non-contrast):
  - Axial and sagittal silicone-only and silicone-suppressed sequences
  - Axial T2 non-FS, T2 FS and T1 non-FS
  - <<No gadolinium required for implant integrity assessment>>

## Step-by-step review workflow

1. **Technical adequacy**: both breasts included, motion, fat suppression uniformity, subtraction registration, bolus timing
2. **MIP survey** for asymmetric enhancing foci
3. **Source DCE + subtractions** in 3 planes — classify each lesion as focus, mass, or NME
4. **Kinetics** with ROI on the most suspicious solid component (avoid necrosis/cyst)
5. **Multiparametric correlation**: T2, T1 non-FS, DWI/ADC
6. **Associated findings**: nipple, skin, chest wall, internal mammary and axillary nodes
7. **Correlate** with mammography/ultrasound and priors
8. **Assign BI-RADS** and clear management recommendation
9. **Measure** longest dimension on the phase/sequence of maximal extent; document multifocal/multicentric/contralateral disease

## BI-RADS MRI lexicon

### Background descriptors

- **Fibroglandular tissue (FGT)**: almost entirely fat / scattered / heterogeneous / extreme
- **Background parenchymal enhancement (BPE)**: minimal / mild / moderate / marked
  - Moderate/marked BPE reduces specificity — consider cycle timing and hormonal therapy

### Lesion types

- **Focus**: <<enhancing dot <5 mm without discernible morphology>>
- **Mass**: 3D space-occupying lesion
- **Non-mass enhancement (NME)**: enhancement without a mass effect

### Mass descriptors

| Feature | Options |
|---|---|
| Shape | Round / oval / irregular |
| Margin | Circumscribed / irregular / spiculated |
| Internal enhancement | Homogeneous / heterogeneous / rim / dark internal septations |
| T2 signal | Markedly hyperintense / hyperintense / iso / hypo |

### NME descriptors

| Feature | Options |
|---|---|
| Distribution | Focal / linear / segmental / regional / multiple regions / diffuse |
| Internal enhancement | Homogeneous / heterogeneous / clumped / **clustered ring** (high PPV) |
| Symmetry | Unilateral vs bilateral |

### Typical malignant patterns

- **Mass**: irregular/spiculated, rim or heterogeneous enhancement, rapid initial + plateau/washout, often low T2, restricted diffusion
- **NME**: <<segmental or linear distribution with clumped or clustered-ring internal enhancement strongly suggests malignancy (often DCIS)>>

### Typical benign patterns

- **Fibroadenoma**: oval, circumscribed, high T2, <<dark internal septations on T2 are a classic feature of fibroadenoma>>, persistent kinetics
- **Cyst**: markedly T2 hyperintense, thin/no wall enhancement
- **Intramammary lymph node**: reniform, fatty hilum, homogeneous enhancement

## Diffusion-weighted imaging

- Adjunct, never overruling malignant morphology
- ADC thresholds (overlap exists):
  - <<ADC ≤1.0 ×10⁻³ mm²/s: suspicious>>
  - ADC 1.0–1.3 ×10⁻³ mm²/s: indeterminate
  - <<ADC ≥1.3 ×10⁻³ mm²/s: more likely benign>>
- Restricted diffusion = high signal on high b-value image **and** low ADC

## Associated findings to actively check

- Nipple retraction/inversion
- Skin thickening, oedema
- Chest wall and pectoralis involvement
- Lymph nodes — <<cortical thickness >3 mm, loss of fatty hilum, round shape or irregular margin are suspicious nodal features>>

## Common pitfalls

- **High BPE** → false positives; reschedule premenopausal women days 7–14
- **Motion/misregistration** on subtraction; always check source images
- **Inadequate fat suppression** at 3T or near clips → prefer Dixon
- **Implant rupture** — do not rely on DCE; use silicone-specific sequences
- **MRI-only lesion** — always perform meticulous second-look ultrasound before MR-guided biopsy

## Kinetic analysis (BI-RADS)

### Initial phase (first ~2 minutes)

| Curve | Signal intensity rise |
|---|---|
| Slow | <<<50% SI increase>> |
| Medium | <<50–100% SI increase>> |
| Rapid | <<>100% SI increase>> |

### Delayed phase

- **Type I — persistent**: continued rise (more often benign)
- **Type II — plateau**: stable (indeterminate)
- **Type III — washout**: declining (more often malignant)

Morphology outweighs kinetics in specificity; never downgrade a morphologically suspicious lesion solely because of persistent kinetics.

## BI-RADS assessment and management

| Category | Meaning | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Incomplete | Recall / prior comparison |
| 1 | Negative | Routine care |
| 2 | Benign | Routine care |
| 3 | Probably benign | <<<2% malignancy risk; MRI follow-up at 6 months, then 12 and 24 months if stable>> |
| 4 | Suspicious | <<Biopsy recommended; subdivided 4A (low), 4B (moderate), 4C (high) suspicion>> |
| 5 | Highly suggestive | <<≥95% malignancy risk; tissue diagnosis and treatment planning>> |
| 6 | Known malignancy | Extent / response assessment |

## Size and staging

- Report **longest diameter of the enhancing invasive component**
- Describe contiguous NME (likely DCIS) separately when it changes surgery
- For multifocal/multicentric disease: list each focus with size, quadrant, distance to nipple, inter-lesion distances

## Neoadjuvant therapy response

- Use **RECIST 1.1** with consistent plane/sequence
- Early functional response: decreasing enhancement and rising ADC precede size reduction
- End-of-therapy: radiologic complete response correlates best with pCR in **HER2+ and triple-negative** subtypes; residual non-enhancing fibrosis may remain

## Special situations

### Post-treatment breast

- Lumpectomy bed rim enhancement common up to 6–18 months
- Fat necrosis: T1/T2-bright internal fat, non-restricted diffusion
- Post-radiotherapy: diffuse oedema and increased BPE for 6–12 months
- Suspect recurrence with new/enlarging spiculated mass, washout kinetics, or clustered-ring NME at/near scar

### Implants

- **Intracapsular rupture**: <<“linguine sign” — wavy collapsed shell within silicone — indicates intracapsular rupture>>
- **Extracapsular rupture**: silicone outside the fibrous capsule, in tissues or axilla
- Use silicone-only and silicone-suppressed sequences; no gadolinium needed

## MR-guided core biopsy (outline)

1. Plan from diagnostic MRI; consent including gadolinium and clip
2. Prone position, breast compression in grid device
3. Localiser → pre-contrast T1 → DCE to confirm enhancement
4. Plan trajectory; introduce coaxial guide under intermittent MRI
5. Vacuum-assisted samples (commonly <<9–11G vacuum-assisted device>>)
6. Place biopsy marker clip; document with limited MRI or mammogram
7. Compress for haemostasis; correlate histology — if discordant, **re-biopsy or excise**

- Coagulation thresholds (local): <<platelets >50 ×10⁹/L and INR <1.5 for MR-guided biopsy>>

## Report structure

1. Indication and clinical context
2. Technique (field strength, coil, sequences, contrast agent/dose)
3. FGT amount and BPE level
4. Side-specific findings: type, descriptors, size, kinetics, DWI
5. Associated features (skin, nipple, chest wall, nodes)
6. Impression with **BI-RADS per side/lesion**
7. Clear recommendation: second-look US, biopsy modality, follow-up interval

## Safety and contrast stewardship

- No ionising radiation
- Macrocyclic gadolinium; 0.1 mmol/kg at 2 mL/s
- Extravasation: stop injection, elevate, cold compress, document and monitor
- Communicate incidental findings appropriately; avoid biopsying benign background phenomena
